# FIFA World Cup 2026 Schedule: Dates, Times, Results and Venues
The FIFA World Cup 2026 is the biggest soccer tournament ever played. Three countries host it together for the first time: the United States, Canada, and Mexico.
The tournament runs from June 11 to July 19, 2026. It features 48 teams, 104 matches, and 16 stadiums spread across North America. That is 40 more games than the 2022 World Cup in Qatar.
The competition has now reached the semifinals, and only four teams are left in the race: France, Spain, England, and Argentina. France plays Spain on July 14, and England plays Argentina on July 15. Both semifinals kick off at 3:00 p.m. ET.
Below is every fixture with dates, kickoff times in Eastern Time (ET), results, and host venues, updated through the latest round.
