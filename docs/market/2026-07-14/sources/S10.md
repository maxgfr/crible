## Who outgrows Simply Wall St
Simply Wall St has genuine strengths: its visual snowflake design makes financial data accessible to investors who find raw numbers intimidating, and its narrative summaries explain what the metrics mean in plain language. For investors just starting out, it is one of the friendlier onboarding experiences in retail investing tools.
But three types of investors consistently look for alternatives:
1. Investors who want raw data, not simplified scores. Simply Wall St distils financial metrics into a five-axis snowflake and colour-coded signals. This abstraction helps beginners but removes the control that more experienced investors want. When a stock shows as "Overvalued" in Simply Wall St, you can't easily see which metrics drove that conclusion or override it with your own assumptions.
2. Investors who need systematic screening. Simply Wall St is built for stock discovery and monitoring — browsing individual stocks, watchlisting, and checking on holdings. It is not built for systematic screening: "find all companies in Europe with P/E below 12, ROE above 15%, and debt/equity below 0.8." The screener exists but is limited; building repeatable filter-based strategies is not the product's design intent.
3. Investors who focus on European small caps. Simply Wall St's global coverage is broad in name count, but fundamental data quality for European small and microcap companies — particularly on alternative markets — is inconsistent. The visual approach also makes it harder to assess data quality than raw number tools where blank values are immediately visible.

## Simply Wall St vs. ScreenerHero: a direct comparison
The tools represent different philosophies:
### Philosophy
Simply Wall St makes investing accessible. It translates raw financial data into visual narratives: "This company has a healthy balance sheet," "Earnings are expected to grow 15% next year," "The stock appears overvalued." The snowflake gives a five-second read of a company's profile. This is genuinely valuable for investors who are learning how to evaluate companies.
ScreenerHero is built for investors who already know what they're looking for. You set raw criteria — P/E below 12, ROE above 15%, EV/EBITDA below 8, positive operating margin — and get a list of companies that meet them. There are no narratives, no scores, no simplified signals. You decide what matters and apply it systematically.
Neither approach is better in absolute terms — they serve different stages and styles of investing.
### Coverage
Simply Wall St: Global, but data quality for European small caps and alternative markets is inconsistent. The visual approach makes data gaps less visible — a missing value might appear as a neutral score rather than an obvious blank.
ScreenerHero: All European exchanges including alternative markets with reliable fundamental data for companies down to microcap level. Data gaps appear as blank filters — immediately visible.

## When Simply Wall St is still the right choice
If you're a beginner. Simply Wall St's narrative approach is the best onboarding experience in retail investing tools. If the goal is to understand what financial metrics mean — not to build systematic screens — the snowflake and plain-language explanations genuinely help.
